2.1.3 Southern Methodist University Heat flow database (Blackwell et al., 2011)

Southern Methodist University Heat flow database (SMUH) was created through measuring temperatures of 99,044 wells across the US (fig. 4) (Blackwell et al., 2011). Included in the SMUH for each well is a calculated geothermal gradient (bottom hole temperature-Harrison correction). Blackwell et al. (2011) produced an interpolation of the heat flow data (fig. 5) but not of the geothermal gradients and the data of the interpolations was not readily availible. Robertson et al. (2021) generated a new interpolation of the geothermal gradient data across the US (fig. 6); this interpolation forms the geothermal gradient component of the coupled methodology to estimate subsurface temperature (the at-formation-depth temperature).
