3.2 Geochemical modelling

Typically as the temperature of the brine increases the volume of CO2 that can dissolve decreases. This is balanced by the pressure effect which results in greater volumes of CO2 dissolving at higher pressures. There are significant variations in the CO2 solubility for brines at similar depths that reflect both spatial variations in geothermal gradients and chemical differences between brines.
For the geochemical modelling we present the predicted change in mineral volume (fig. 19), volume (fig. 20) and value (fig. 21) of the storable CO2 for all the samples from Texas. We used a reservoir lithology comprised of calcite (1 mole), dolomite (1 mole) and halite (1 mole). The dimensions of the theoretical reservoir of influence were 200m radius * 100m height * 0.15 saturated porosity.
